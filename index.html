Growth Opportunities

Where Visitors Are Slipping Away — and How to Fix It

The items below are listed in order of impact. Think of each one as a leaky bucket — visitors are arriving, but something is causing them to leave before they contact you. Patching each leak compounds the result.

01
Fix Immediately · Est. 10 min

The Phone Number on Your Homepage Dials the Wrong Number

This is the single most urgent item in this entire audit. Your homepage displays the number (916) 316-2642, but when a visitor on their phone taps that number to call, it actually dials (916) 802-2399 — a completely different number.

For every mobile visitor who taps "call" — which is how most people contact businesses today — they are being sent to the wrong number. This is a direct, measurable loss of leads every single day the site stays as-is.

Why this happens: In Wix, the visible text and the actual phone link behind it are set separately. At some point the displayed number was updated, but the underlying "tap-to-call" link was not.
The fix: Open the Wix editor, click the phone number element, click "Link" or the phone icon, then update the dialing number to match what's displayed. Test by tapping it on your own phone before saving.
02
Fix Immediately · Est. 30 min

Your Email Address Is Quietly Costing You Jobs

The contact email behind your website is paynterconstruction@yahoo.com. When prospective customers — especially homeowners considering a $20,000–$80,000 ADU or deck project — see or receive emails from a Yahoo address, it unconsciously signals a smaller, less established operation than you actually are.

Your website already shows info@paynterconstruction.com, which looks great. The goal is to make that the real working email address.

Why it matters: Home improvement is a high-trust purchase. People are inviting a contractor onto their property and writing large checks. Every detail — including the email domain — signals professionalism and builds (or erodes) that trust.
The fix (~$6/month): Set up Google Workspace for your domain. You'll get a real info@paynterconstruction.com inbox that works through Gmail. Then update the email link in Wix to point to the new address. The entire setup takes about 30 minutes.
03
High Impact · Est. 15 min

There's No "Next Step" Button in Your Hero Section

When someone lands on your homepage, the very first thing they see is a beautiful deck photo — which is excellent. But there's no clear button telling them what to do next. A small floating contact box exists, but it blends into the background.

Studies consistently show that most visitors decide whether to take action within the first 5–10 seconds of landing on a page. If they don't immediately see a button or a clear invitation to reach out, many will simply leave and check the next contractor on their list.

The fix: Add a single, prominent button overlaid on the hero photo — something like "Get a Free Estimate" in a warm amber or white color that stands out against the deck photo. Link it directly to your contact section. This single change is one of the highest-return improvements any contractor website can make.
04
High Impact · Est. 5 min

Your Headline Introduces the Business — But Not the Benefit

The large text displayed over your hero photo reads "PAYNTER CONSTRUCTION, INC." This is your company name — which is important — but your company name alone doesn't answer the question your visitor is actually asking: Can these people build exactly what I'm envisioning, and can I trust them?

The most effective contractor headlines speak directly to what the homeowner gets, not just who the company is.

Current "PAYNTER CONSTRUCTION, INC. — Decks, Sheds, Awnings & More"
Suggested "Beautifully Built Decks, Sheds & ADUs — Trusted Across Placer County"
The company name still lives in the logo and throughout the site. The hero headline is premium real estate — use it to speak to the customer's desire, not just to reintroduce yourself.
05
High Impact · Est. 15 min

Your Reviews Aren't Visible — And Reviews Sell for You 24/7

Homeowners researching contractors almost always check reviews before making contact. Your current website has no star ratings, no Google review count, and no customer testimonials visible above the fold (or easily findable at all).

This is one of the fastest ways to increase conversion without changing anything else — because reviews do the persuading for you. A line as simple as "★★★★★ Rated 4.9 on Google · 47 Reviews" near the top of the page can dramatically increase the percentage of visitors who reach out.

The fix: Install the free "Google Reviews" widget from the Wix App Market. It pulls your real reviews automatically and displays them on your site. Also add a short social proof line near your phone number at the top of the page showing your star rating and review count.
06
This Month · Est. 10 min

Your Contact Form Is Asking for Too Much, Too Soon

Your current contact form has six fields: First Name, Last Name, Email, Message, Phone, and Address. For someone who just discovered your business, that's a lot to ask for in a first interaction — and research consistently shows that every additional form field reduces the number of people who complete and submit it.

Current (6 fields) First Name · Last Name · Email · Message · Phone · Address
Suggested (3 fields) Name · Phone Number · "What can we help you build?"
You'll collect the address during the estimate call. The goal of the website form is simply to start the conversation — keep the bar low.
07
This Month · Est. 2 min

One Word in Your Tagline Is Working Against You

Your services page opens with: "Local Family Owned Small Construction ADU Specialists." The word "small" is meant to convey boutique and personal — and that's a genuinely great message. But the word itself triggers a hesitation in a homeowner's mind: Can they handle my project?

Current "Local Family Owned Small Construction ADU Specialists"
Suggested "Local Family Owned Boutique Construction — ADU Specialists"
08
60–90 Days

Each Service Deserves Its Own Page

Currently, every service — Decks, Sheds, Tiny Homes, and Awnings — lives on a single /services page. This works fine for a visitor who's browsing. But when someone searches specifically for "deck builder Loomis CA" or "ADU contractor Placer County," having a dedicated page for each service dramatically improves both your search ranking and the likelihood that the visitor fills out a form (because the page speaks directly to their need).

The opportunity: Create individual pages for /decks, /sheds, /adu-tiny-homes, and /awnings. Each page should have service-specific photos, copy, and a "Request a Free Estimate for Your [Deck]" button. This is the single biggest long-term driver of organic search traffic and conversion.
